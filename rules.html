Welcome to Ruthless Roleplay!

We are thrilled to have you join our community. At Ruthless Roleplay, we strive to create an immersive and high-quality roleplaying experience that brings our world to life. The rules outlined below are in place to ensure that our culture, values, and goals are upheld. These guidelines help maintain an environment where everyone can enjoy authentic and meaningful roleplay, free from disruption.

By following these rules, we can all contribute to the success of our community and ensure that every member has the opportunity to engage in the best possible roleplay experience.

Community Rules
Respect One Another
Treat all members of the server community with dignity, kindness, and respect, regardless of their in-game characters or backgrounds. Handle any disagreements in a civil and mature manner, focusing on constructive communication rather than personal attacks.
Respect Everyone's Roleplay
Value the unique roleplaying style of each player. Contribute positively to the server's overall narrative by engaging respectfully with others' storylines and avoiding actions that diminish their roleplay experience.
No Roleplay Evading
When you are involved in a roleplay scenario, you are expected to engage with it meaningfully and accept the consequences of your character's actions. Avoid logging off or finding ways to circumvent ongoing roleplay. If you are unable to participate fully for OOC reasons, communicate this respectfully in an appropriate OOC manner (e.g., using a designated OOC chat or a ticket).
No Hate Speech
Absolutely no discriminatory language targeting individuals or groups based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic will be tolerated. Violations of this rule will result in severe consequences.
No Harassment
Harassment of any kind, including but not limited to personal attacks, stalking, threats, and unwanted attention, is strictly prohibited both in-game and in community spaces like Discord. Report any instances of harassment to the staff immediately.
No Off-Server Misconduct
Maintain respectful and positive behavior in all online interactions related to the server, including in Discord and other community platforms. Actions taken outside the game that significantly impact the server community may be subject to staff review.
Zero Tolerance for Toxic Behavior
Any disruptive actions that negatively impact the enjoyment of the community as a whole, such as excessive negativity, constant complaining, or creating unnecessary drama, will not be tolerated.
No Promoting Other Servers
Do not advertise or recruit for other FiveM communities or servers without explicit permission from the server staff.
Adult Server (18+ Only)
This server contains mature themes and content that may not be suitable for minors. Participation is restricted to individuals who are 18 years of age or older.
Bans
Bans are issued at the discretion of the server staff based on the severity and impact of the rule violation. Staff decisions regarding bans are considered final.
No Backseat Admins
Players who are not staff members should not attempt to enforce server rules or give administrative directions to other players. If you believe a rule has been broken, report it to the staff through the proper channels.
Ban Appeals
If you believe you have been unfairly banned, follow the designated ban appeal procedure outlined by the server. Staff will review appeals, but their final decision remains binding.
Ban Evasion
Attempting to bypass a server ban by using alternative accounts or methods will result in permanent and potentially more severe consequences.
Leave Drama Outside
Keep personal conflicts and issues that originate outside of the server's roleplay environment out of in-game interactions and community communication channels.
No Bug Exploits
Intentionally using glitches, bugs, or unintended game mechanics to gain an unfair advantage is strictly prohibited. If you discover a bug, report it to the staff immediately rather than exploiting it.
Report Offenses
If you witness another player breaking a server rule, do not attempt to intervene yourself. Instead, report the incident to the server staff through the appropriate channels (e.g., in-game tickets, Discord reports) so they can handle it effectively.
Leave Beefs from Previous Servers
Do not bring conflicts, grudges, or negative history from other FiveM servers into this community. Treat everyone within this server as a new interaction.
No Excessive Sexual Verbal Content
Avoid making inappropriate or overly explicit sexual comments in public chat or voice channels that may make other players uncomfortable. While mature themes are present, focus on roleplay rather than explicit verbal content.
General Rules
Roleplay First
In every situation, your primary focus should be on creating and engaging in immersive roleplay. Respond to roleplay initiated by others and prioritize storytelling over other game mechanics.
In-Game Harassment
While intense conflict is a part of roleplay, it should never be used as an excuse for personal attacks or OOC harassment. All intense roleplay scenarios require mutual respect and a level of implied or explicit consent between the involved players.
No "Cry Baby" / "Iyakin" Mentality
Embrace the outcomes of your roleplay, both positive and negative. Avoid complaining excessively about in-game events. If you have a legitimate Out-Of-Character (OOC) issue or believe a rule has been broken, report it via the ticket system rather than reacting negatively in character.
Don't Turn Things Into a Ticket War
Use the ticket system constructively to report rule violations or seek staff assistance with legitimate issues. Avoid creating multiple unnecessary tickets or arguing excessively with staff decisions within the ticket system.
No Breaking Character (OOC in RP)
During roleplay scenes, you should remain in character at all times. Avoid using Out-Of-Character (OOC) information or speaking about OOC matters in a way that breaks immersion for yourself and others. Exceptions are typically made only for severe rule violations or when explicitly communicating with staff.
No Exploiting
Abusing any game mechanics, glitches, or loopholes to gain an unfair advantage over other players or to circumvent intended gameplay is strictly prohibited. This includes but is not limited to exploiting server scripts, animations, or game physics.
Value Your Life (NVL)
Roleplay your character with a realistic sense of self-preservation. Avoid engaging in actions that would realistically lead to serious injury or death without a valid in-character reason and demonstrating appropriate fear for your character's well-being.
No Metagaming
Do not use Out-Of-Character (OOC) information that your character has not learned in-character. This includes information gained from streams, Discord, or other OOC sources. All In-Character (IC) communication and knowledge must be obtained through in-game interactions.
No Stream Sniping or Using Stream Information IC
Watching another player's stream and using information you gain from it in your own roleplay is strictly prohibited as it constitutes metagaming and can severely impact the fairness and immersion of the roleplay environment.
No Powergaming
Do not perform actions that force outcomes on other characters without giving them a reasonable opportunity to react or roleplay the situation. This includes actions that are unrealistic or god-like in nature. For example, instantly knowing someone's thoughts or performing actions that are physically impossible for your character.
Fail RP (Failure to Roleplay)
Any actions that break the immersion of the roleplay environment, disrespect the established rules, or demonstrate a lack of understanding of basic roleplaying principles may be considered Fail RP. This can include unrealistic actions, illogical behavior, or a general disregard for the roleplay experience of others. Such actions may lead to removal from the scene or other consequences.
No "Dirty Whitelisted" RP
Players in whitelisted roles (e.g., law enforcement, EMS) must not abuse their access to resources, information, or abilities for personal gain in illegal activities or to unfairly benefit their criminal characters or associates without a strong and justifiable in-character reason that is appropriately roleplayed.
Roleplay Over Gunplay / Unnecessary Low-Quality Violence
Prioritize engaging in meaningful roleplay and dialogue over resorting to violence. Gunplay and other violent actions should only be a last resort and must be preceded by significant in-character reasoning and narrative buildup. Attempt to de-escalate situations through communication whenever possible.
Violence Must Have Meaning
Any act of violence should be driven by a compelling in-character story or motivation, not based on petty reasons, impulsiveness, or a desire for random conflict.
No Body Dumping, Forcing Respawn, or Forced Character Kills (CKs)
To avoid the consequences of roleplay or to end a character without proper justification, players are prohibited from actions such as disposing of bodies in unrealistic ways, intentionally forcing their character to respawn, or unilaterally deciding to permanently kill another player's character. Character Kills (CKs) require explicit staff approval and must be supported by significant and well-developed roleplay.
Revenge RP
Seeking immediate retaliation for negative roleplay outcomes (e.g., being robbed, arrested, or injured) without allowing for meaningful in-character development and planning is discouraged. Revenge roleplay should be approached with a focus on creating a compelling story rather than immediate, impulsive action.
No Random Deathmatching (RDM)
Attacking or killing another player without any prior meaningful roleplay interaction or a valid in-character reason is strictly prohibited. There must be a clear and logical in-character justification for initiating violence against another individual.
Conflicts
In-Game Harassment
While intense conflict is a part of roleplay, it should never be used as an excuse for personal attacks or repeated discomfort towards another player. All intense roleplay scenarios require mutual respect and a level of implied or explicit consent between the involved players. Staff will investigate any claims of Out-Of-Character (OOC) driven harassment that occurs under the guise of in-character conflict.
Roleplay Over Gunplay / Unnecessary Low-Quality Violence
Even in conflict situations, prioritize engaging in meaningful roleplay and dialogue over resorting to violence. Gunplay and other violent actions should only be a last resort and must be preceded by significant in-character reasoning and narrative buildup. Attempt to de-escalate situations through communication whenever possible. Violence should not be based on petty or impulsive reasons.
Revenge RP
Seeking immediate retaliation for negative conflict outcomes without allowing for meaningful in-character development and planning is discouraged. Revenge roleplay should be approached with a focus on creating a compelling story rather than immediate, impulsive action.
Illegal Activities
Criminal Mindset
When roleplaying a criminal character, you are expected to act in a manner consistent with that role. This includes being discreet in your actions and understanding that a primary goal of criminal activity is to avoid detection and apprehension by law enforcement.
No Exploiting
Prohibited for any advantage, including in illegal activities. Examples include combat logging (quitting during a conflict or illegal activity), glitch abuse, camping respawn points, initiating major crimes immediately before or after a server restart to avoid consequences, emote abuse to gain an unfair advantage, illegal item transfers between characters outside of legitimate roleplay, and respawn abuse to avoid capture or other consequences.
No "Dirty Whitelisted" RP
Players in whitelisted roles (e.g., law enforcement, EMS) must not abuse their access to resources, information, or abilities to aid their own or others' illegal activities or to gain unfair advantages in conflicts without a strong and justifiable in-character reason that is appropriately roleplayed and consistent with their faction's guidelines.
No Body Dumping, Forcing Respawn, or Forced Character Kills (CKs)
To avoid the consequences of illegal actions, players are prohibited from disposing of bodies in unrealistic ways, intentionally forcing their character to respawn after being incapacitated, or unilaterally deciding to permanently kill another player's character involved in the illegal activity. Character Kills (CKs) related to illegal activities require explicit staff approval and must be supported by significant and well-developed roleplay.
Major Crimes Near Restart
Initiating major illegal activities (e.g., store robberies, bank heists) within 30 minutes before or after a scheduled server restart is prohibited to prevent using the restart as a means of escaping consequences or disrupting server stability. Furthermore, upon receiving a server restart notification, players involved in ongoing crimes should make reasonable efforts to conclude the scenario through roleplay before the restart occurs, and any rewards or consequences from illegal activities that are not completed prior to server restart will be voided at staff discretion.
No "Palit Ulo" (No Trading Heads/Scapegoating)
Players who are directly involved in committing a crime must face the in-character consequences of their actions. Attempting to shift blame onto another individual or having someone else take responsibility for your crimes without legitimate in-character legal proceedings (such as utilizing Department of Justice roleplay for defense or plea bargains) is prohibited. The principle is: if your character did the crime, your character faces the consequences.
Heists and Robberies - Focus on Escape
The primary roleplay concept for heists and robberies should center around the planning and execution of a viable escape plan. Unnecessary casualties or killings during these scenarios should be avoided unless they arise from legitimate self-defense or as a last resort within the established roleplay to ensure the survival of the involved criminals.
Robbery Participant Limits
Generally, expect limits such as 2-4 criminals for small-scale robberies (e.g., convenience stores, ATMs), 3-5 criminals for medium-scale robberies (e.g., jewelry stores, smaller banks), and 4-6 or potentially more for large-scale robberies (e.g., major banks, heists, as defined by server rules).
Small-Time Illegal Activity Limit
For smaller-scale illegal activities such as house robberies and the sale of drugs, the maximum number of criminal participants allowed is 4. This is to encourage more discreet and manageable scenarios.
Hostage-Taking Rules
In robbery roleplay scenarios, such as ATM and store robberies, taking a person hostage is permitted. However, outside of these direct robbery situations, hostage-taking is not allowed for any other reason.
Demand Limitations
  • 1 hostage = 2 demands
  • 2 hostages = 3 demands
  • 3 or more hostages = 3 demands (maximum)
  • Any demand that is considered "stupid" or "unfair" will not be honored. Valid demands are limited to the following:
    • No motorcycle unit
    • No helicopter unit
    • Free Passage
    • No Pitting
    • No Spikes
    • Cash Demand: Up to $1000 per hostage, regardless of who they are.
All demands will be voided under the following conditions
  • The robbers perform more than 3 "strats" or "jumps."
  • The robbers perform more than 2 vehicle switches.
  • The chase exceeds 15 minutes.
Law Enforcement Response
The number of law enforcement officers responding to any criminal activity will be determined by the internal policies, staffing levels, and dispatch procedures of the law enforcement department within the server. Police officers will not open fire unless they are directly threatened with lethal force or if the situation realistically calls for the use of deadly force to protect themselves or others (e.g., an active shooter scenario).
